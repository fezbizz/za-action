What is Happening in South Africa?

📊 The Numbers

Estimates suggest between 3–8 million undocumented immigrants are in South Africa. Our border management and asylum system are under severe strain, with over 180,000 asylum applications pending in some periods.

🏘️ Community Impact

Overcrowded informal settlements, pressure on clinics and schools, strain on housing lists, and competition in the informal economy — especially in Gauteng, Western Cape, and KZN hotspots.

⚖️ Government Response

The Border Management Authority (BMA) was launched. Operations like Vala Umgodi target illegal mining and associated undocumented foreign nationals. The Asylum Seeker Processing backlog remains a challenge.

📰 Recent Developments

Policy shifts include increased border patrols, biometric systems at ports of entry, and public consultations on immigration reform. Civil movements like Operation Dudula have raised public debate.

✅ Facts vs Myths

❌ Myth

"All foreign nationals in SA are illegal."

✅ Fact

Some are documented asylum seekers, legal residents, or work-permit holders — but that doesn't change the fact that undocumented / illegal entry and stay is a serious problem. The focus should be on enforcing our laws.

❌ Myth

"There's nothing we can do as ordinary citizens."

✅ Fact

You can report through legal channels, join community policing forums, participate in ward committees, volunteer with NGOs, and hold government accountable through public participation.

🔥 How You Can Take Action

1

Get Informed

Follow parliamentary portfolio committee meetings on immigration. Read the Immigration Act. Understand the difference between a refugee, asylum seeker, and economic migrant.

2

Join a CPF

Community Policing Forums exist in every precinct. Join yours. This is a legal, structured way to participate in community safety.

3

Report Legally

Use official SAPS and Home Affairs channels to report illegal activities. Do not take the law into your own hands — vigilantism harms our democracy.

4

Speak Up Online

Share verified information. Create TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube content that informs peers. Call out misinformation.

5

Engage Politically

Attend ward meetings. Submit public comments on proposed laws. Join political parties that align with your views on immigration and border control.

6

Volunteer

Organisations working on documentation, legal aid, and community safety need volunteers. Your time and skills make a difference.

📢 Official Reporting Channels

🚔 SAPS Crime Stop

08600 10111

SMS 32211 or report at your nearest police station. You can remain anonymous.

🪪 Home Affairs

0800 60 11 90

National Contact Centre. Report undocumented foreign nationals or immigration fraud.

🛂 Border Management Authority

Report illegal border crossings and human trafficking. Check the BMA website for regional office contacts.

👷 Employment & Labour

Report employers hiring undocumented workers or violating labour laws. Labour centres across all provinces.

⚠️ Important: Always act within the law. Vigilantism, violence, and harassment of documented foreign nationals is illegal and un-South African. Our Constitution protects everyone in our country, regardless of status.
