# SA Action — 🇿🇦 Youth Information & Mobilization Hub

A resource to inform and motivate young South Africans around immigration, border control, and civic participation.

## What's This About?

South Africa faces significant challenges around undocumented immigration — pressure on public services, housing, jobs, and infrastructure. This project aims to:

- **Inform** young South Africans with facts (not hearsay)
- **Educate** on legal channels: Home Affairs processes, asylum-seeker procedures, border management
- **Mobilize** civic participation: reporting channels, community patrols, activism, political engagement
- **Combat misinformation** with verified sources

## Key Topics to Cover

### 1. The Scale of the Issue
- Estimates of undocumented immigrants in SA (various sources: 3–8 million)
- Pressure on Gauteng, Western Cape, KZN townships & informal settlements
- Impact on housing, healthcare, education, and informal economy

### 2. Government Response
- Home Affairs: Border Management Authority (BMA) operations
- Operation Vala Umgodi / Operation Dudula movement
- Digital border management initiatives
- Asylum-seeker processing backlog
- 2024/2025 policy developments

### 3. What Young South Africans Can Do
- Join or form community safety structures (within the law)
- Report illegal activities via SAPS, Home Affairs hotlines
- Volunteer with civil society organizations
- Participate in public comment on immigration policy
- Run for local ward committees / community policing forums
- Create content and awareness on social media
